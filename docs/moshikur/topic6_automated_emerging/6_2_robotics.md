# Robotics

Robotics is a branch of computer science that involves designing, building, and operating robots. Robots are machines that can perform tasks on their own or with some human control.

[![Illustration of a robot holding a lock, surrounded by icons of gears, a robotic arm, and a microprocessor, with the word 'ROBOTICS' prominently displayed.](images/topic6_automated_emerging/6_2_robotics/img_001.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/image.png?ssl=1)

---

### **What Are Robots?**

Robots are machines created to make tasks easier for humans. They can follow instructions, perform repetitive jobs, and even make some decisions based on the data they receive.

---

### **Examples of Robotics in Real Life**

1. **Factory Equipment (Industry):**
   - Robots are widely used in factories to perform repetitive tasks like assembling cars or packaging goods.
   - **Example:** Robotic arms weld car parts with precision and speed.
2. **Domestic Robots:**
   - These are robots used at home to assist with tasks like cleaning or cooking.
   - **Example:** Robotic vacuums (like Roomba) automatically clean the floors.
3. **Drones:**
   - Drones are flying robots often used for surveillance, photography, or delivery.
   - **Example:** Delivery drones transport packages to hard-to-reach areas.

---

### **Why Are Robots Important?**

Robots are important because they:

- Work faster and more accurately than humans.
- Perform tasks in dangerous environments where humans cannot go.
- Save time and reduce costs in industries.

## **Characteristics of Robots**

Robots are unique because they combine mechanical and electrical systems with programmable capabilities. Let’s break down the key characteristics of robots:

---

### **1. Mechanical Structure or Framework**

- **What it is:**  
  The mechanical structure is the physical body of the robot that allows it to move and interact with the environment.
- **Features:**
  - Can include arms, legs, wheels, or grippers.
  - Made from materials like metal, plastic, or alloys.
- **Examples:**
  - A robotic arm used in factories has joints and motors to replicate human arm movements.
  - Drones have propellers as part of their mechanical structure for flying.

---

### **2. Electrical Components**

Electrical components allow robots to sense, process, and act. These include:

#### **a) Sensors**

- **What they do:** Sensors gather information from the environment.
- **Examples:** Light sensors, motion sensors, temperature sensors.
- **Use:** A robotic vacuum uses sensors to detect walls and obstacles.

#### **b) Microprocessors**

- **What they do:** Microprocessors are the “brains” of the robot. They process the data received from sensors and make decisions.
- **Example:** A robot might decide to stop moving if a sensor detects an obstacle.

#### **c) Actuators**

- **What they do:** Actuators are the “muscles” of the robot. They execute actions like moving wheels, rotating arms, or gripping objects.
- **Example:** Actuators allow a robotic gripper to pick up items on a factory conveyor belt.

---

### **3. Programmable**

- **What it means:**  
  Robots are programmable, meaning they can be given specific instructions to perform tasks.
- **Examples:**
  - A robotic arm is programmed to weld car parts in an assembly line.
  - Drones are programmed to deliver packages to specific locations.
- **Why it’s important:**
  - Programming gives robots flexibility to adapt to different tasks.
  - Robots can perform tasks automatically without constant human input.

---

### **How These Characteristics Work Together**

Here’s how the characteristics combine in a robot:

1. **Sensors** detect changes in the environment (e.g., a motion sensor detects movement).
2. **Microprocessors** analyze the data from sensors and make decisions (e.g., the robot decides to move).
3. **Actuators** carry out the action (e.g., the robot moves forward using its wheels).

---

### **Real-Life Example: Robotic Vacuum Cleaner**

1. **Mechanical Structure:** The vacuum has wheels for movement and brushes to sweep the floor.
2. **Sensors:** Detect dirt and obstacles.
3. **Microprocessors:** Decide the path to follow and when to turn or stop.
4. **Actuators:** Control the movement of wheels and the suction motor to clean the floor.

## **Roles Robots Can Perform**

Robots are used in many areas to assist humans by performing tasks that are repetitive, dangerous, or require precision. Below are the key roles robots can perform in different fields:

#### **1. Industry**

- **Role:** Robots are used to assemble, package, or inspect products in factories.
- **Examples:**
  - Robotic arms assemble car parts or weld frames.
  - Robots inspect electronic components for quality assurance.

#### **2. Transport**

- **Role:** Robots and AI systems are used to automate vehicles and manage transport systems.
- **Examples:**
  - Self-driving cars use sensors to navigate roads safely.
  - Automated trains operate on set schedules with minimal human intervention.

#### **3. Agriculture**

- **Role:** Robots help in planting, harvesting, and monitoring crops.
- **Examples:**
  - Drones survey fields and identify areas needing water or fertilizer.
  - Robots automatically pick fruits or plant seeds.

#### **4. Medicine**

- **Role:** Robots assist doctors in surgeries, diagnostics, and patient care.
- **Examples:**
  - Surgical robots provide precision and control during delicate operations.
  - Robots deliver medicine and monitor patient health in hospitals.

#### **5. Domestic Settings**

- **Role:** Robots perform household tasks to save time and effort for humans.
- **Examples:**
  - Robotic vacuums clean floors automatically.
  - Smart kitchen appliances prepare food.

#### **6. Entertainment**

- **Role:** Robots are used in interactive experiences and creative industries.
- **Examples:**
  - Animatronics in theme parks simulate lifelike movements.
  - Robotic pets provide companionship and fun.

### **Advantages and Disadvantages of Robots**

#### **Advantages of Using Robots**

1. **Improved Efficiency:**  
   Robots work faster and more accurately than humans, especially in repetitive tasks.
2. **Precision:**  
   Robots perform tasks with consistent accuracy, making them ideal for delicate operations like surgeries.
3. **Safety:**  
   Robots can work in hazardous environments, such as chemical plants or space exploration, reducing risks to human workers.
4. **Cost-Effective (Long-Term):**  
   Though expensive initially, robots reduce long-term labor costs by replacing repetitive human tasks.
5. **Availability:**  
   Robots can work continuously without breaks, increasing productivity.

#### **Disadvantages of Using Robots**

1. **High Initial Cost:**  
   Designing, building, and programming robots can be expensive.
2. **Maintenance:**  
   Robots require regular maintenance, which can also be costly.
3. **Job Loss:**  
   The use of robots in industries can lead to unemployment as they replace human workers in repetitive jobs.
4. **Limited Adaptability:**  
   Robots can only perform tasks they are programmed for and may struggle in unexpected situations.
5. **Dependence on Technology:**  
   A malfunction or power outage can halt the work of robots, causing delays.

### **Examples of Robots in Action**

#### **Case 1: Robots in Factories**

- **Scenario:** A robotic arm assembles car engines on a production line.
- **Process:**
  1. Sensors detect the engine parts on the conveyor belt.
  2. The microprocessor processes this data and decides which part to pick up.
  3. The actuator moves the robotic arm to place the part in its correct position.

#### **Case 2: Robots in Agriculture**

- **Scenario:** A drone monitors a large field for pests and watering needs.
- **Process:**
  1. The drone’s sensors capture real-time data about crop health.
  2. The microprocessor analyzes this data and identifies areas that need attention.
  3. Actuators adjust the drone’s movement to focus on those areas.

#### **Case 3: Robots in Medicine**

- **Scenario:** A surgical robot assists a doctor in performing a delicate procedure.
- **Process:**
  1. The robot follows the doctor’s precise commands.
  2. Sensors ensure the instruments are positioned correctly.
  3. Actuators move surgical tools with extreme precision.
