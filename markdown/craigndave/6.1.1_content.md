# 6.1 Automated systems
*Automated systems – Part 1*

---

### Automated systems
- Technology has led to great advancements in automated systems that require little to no human interaction.
- Automation is used in many areas including:
  - Industry
  - Transport
  - Agriculture
  - Weather
  - Gaming
  - Lighting
  - Science

### Automated systems
- Automated systems usually comprise three distinct components.
- Microprocessors
- Actuators
- Sensors

### Automated systems
- A vast array of different sensors exist – they are used to take measurements from their environmental surroundings.
- We look at sensors in a lot more detail in the following videos:
  - 3.2 Sensors - Part 1
  - 3.2 Sensors - Part 2

### Automated systems
- Sensors provide these measurements as inputs to microprocessors, which then process the information and make decisions based on predefined rules.

### Automated systems
- Decisions made by microprocessors typically result in instructions to control an actuator and make physical changes – e.g., activating an alarm, opening or closing a door or valve, etc.

### Feedback
- These components are often used to create a feedback loop where changes (outputs) from the automated system affect the following set of input values received from the sensors.
