### Algorithmic thinking
- Algorithmic thinking is a way of getting to a solution by identifying the individual steps required.
- By creating a set of rules, an algorithm – when followed precisely – will lead to an answer. For example, in school, we all learn algorithms for doing multiplication.
- If a human (or a computer) follows these rules precisely, the answer to any multiplication problem can be found.

- Abstraction also allows us to realise that:
- The size of the grid is not important.
- The words are not important.
- The individual letters are not important.
- What is important is:
- The word we are currently looking for.
- The letters for that word in the grid.
- All the other letters are irrelevant – this allows us to simplify the grid for the purposes of programming and testing.
