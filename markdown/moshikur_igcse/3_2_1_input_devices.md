# 3.2.1 INPUT Devices

**What is an Input Device?**

An **input device** is hardware that allows us to send data and instructions to a computer. It captures information from the real world, such as text, images, sounds, or actions, and converts it into a format the computer can process. Without input devices, it would be impossible for humans to interact with computers effectively.

**Why are Input Devices Important?**

They allow us to provide instructions to a computer.

They enable us to input data such as text, images, and sounds.

They make computers interactive and easier to use.

### **Manual and Automatic Input Devices**

**Manual input devices** are devices where the **user directly enters data** into the computer by hand. The user controls what data is entered and when it is entered. Examples include a keyboard, mouse, and touch screen.

**Automatic input devices** enter data into the computer **without the user typing it manually**. These devices read data automatically from objects, documents, or the environment. Examples include barcode scanners, QR code scanners, and sensors.

---

### **Difference between Manual and Automatic Input Devices**

| Manual Input Devices | Automatic Input Devices |
| --- | --- |
| Data is entered by the user | Data is captured automatically |
| Requires user action | Minimal or no user action |
| Slower for large amounts of data | Faster for large amounts of data |
| Examples: keyboard, mouse, touch screen | Examples: barcode scanner, QR code scanner, sensors |

# 3.2.1.1 Barcode Scanner

---

[![An illustration of a barcode scanner emitting red laser beams aimed at a barcode with the number 12345678012 beneath it.](images/topic3_hardware/3_2_1_input_devices/img_001.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-40.png?ssl=1)

### What is a Barcode Scanner?

A barcode scanner is an **input device** that reads barcodes and converts them into digital data. A barcode is a set of **black and white lines of varying thickness** that represent numbers or characters. The scanner uses a light source to detect the pattern of the bars and spaces, and the data is then sent to the computer.

---

### Purpose of a Barcode Scanner

The purpose of a barcode scanner is to **quickly and accurately capture data** without typing. This helps reduce errors and speeds up tasks such as sales, stock control, and product identification. Barcode scanners are commonly used in supermarkets, libraries, warehouses, and ticket systems.

---

### How Does a Barcode Scanner Work?

1. The scanner shines a beam of light (often a laser) onto the barcode.
2. The light reflects back differently from the black bars and white spaces.
3. A sensor inside the scanner detects the reflected light pattern.
4. The pattern is converted into an electrical signal.
5. The signal is then translated into digital data (numbers or characters) and sent to the computer system.

---

### Advantages of Barcode Scanners

- **Fast and efficient** – data entry is much quicker than typing.
- **Accurate** – reduces human error.
- **Cost-effective** – barcodes are cheap to print and easy to use.
- **Easy to use** – requires little training.

### Disadvantages of Barcode Scanners

- Barcodes can be **damaged or dirty**, making them hard to read.
- **Limited information** – barcodes usually store only numbers or short codes.
- Requires a **direct line of sight** between the scanner and the barcode.
- Cannot track real-time data on its own – must be part of a larger system.

---

### Uses of Barcode Scanners

- **Retail** – scanning items at checkouts in supermarkets and shops.
- **Libraries** – tracking borrowed and returned books.
- **Warehouses** – managing stock levels.
- **Ticketing systems** – scanning tickets at concerts, events, and transport stations.

# 3.2.1.2 QR Code Scanner

---

[![A QR code featuring a square grid of black and white squares, labeled 'QR Code'.](images/topic3_hardware/3_2_1_input_devices/img_002.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-45.png?ssl=1)

### What is a QR Code Scanner?

A QR (Quick Response) code scanner is an **input device** that reads QR codes and converts them into digital data. A QR code looks like a **square grid filled with small black and white squares**. Unlike barcodes, which only store data in one direction, QR codes store data **horizontally and vertically**, allowing them to hold much more information.

---

### Purpose of a QR Code Scanner

The purpose of a QR code scanner is to **quickly capture large amounts of data** such as website links, payment details, or product information. They are widely used in marketing, retail, transportation, and digital services because they can be scanned by dedicated scanners or even smartphone cameras.

---

### How Does a QR Code Scanner Work?

1. The scanner (or camera) captures an image of the QR code.
2. The QR code is divided into patterns of black and white squares.
3. The scanner software analyses the patterns to identify binary data (0s and 1s).
4. The binary data is converted into readable information such as text, numbers, or a web link.
5. The decoded information is sent to the computer or smartphone application.

---

### Advantages of QR Code Scanners

- **Stores more data** than traditional barcodes.
- Can store **different types of information** (text, links, payments, etc.).
- **Fast and reliable** scanning process.
- Can be scanned by both **special scanners and mobile devices**.

---

### Disadvantages of QR Code Scanners

- **Require a camera or scanner** capable of reading QR codes.
- **Poor lighting or damaged codes** can make scanning difficult.
- Some people may not know how to use QR codes effectively.
- Can be misused by scammers to direct users to unsafe websites.

---

### Uses of QR Code Scanners

- **Retail and restaurants** – scanning product info, menus, and payments.
- **Transportation** – scanning tickets for buses, trains, or flights.
- **Marketing** – linking posters and advertisements to websites.
- **Education** – quick access to resources through QR links.
- **Healthcare** – storing patient or medication information.

# 3.2.1.3 Keyboard

---

[![Illustration of a computer keyboard with keys arranged in a typical layout.](images/topic3_hardware/3_2_1_input_devices/img_003.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-46.png?ssl=1)

A keyboard is an **input device** used to enter text, numbers, and commands into a computer. It consists of keys arranged in a specific layout, most commonly the **QWERTY layout**. Each key press sends a digital signal to the computer, which interprets it as a character or command.

---

### How Does a Keyboard Work?

1. When a key is pressed, an electrical circuit is completed.
2. The keyboard controller detects the key press and generates a signal.
3. The signal is sent to the computer as a **binary code**.
4. The operating system interprets the code and displays the corresponding character or performs the command.

---

### Advantages of Keyboards

- **Fast data entry** for typing large amounts of text.
- **Accurate** if the user is skilled.
- **Widely available** and easy to use.
- Different layouts (QWERTY, AZERTY, DVORAK) available for different needs.

---

### Disadvantages of Keyboards

- **Slow for non-text data entry** (e.g., drawings).
- Can cause **repetitive strain injuries (RSI)** with prolonged use.
- Requires **desk space**, not suitable for small devices.
- Input can be **error-prone** if the user types incorrectly.

---

### Uses of Keyboards

- **Typing documents** such as essays, reports, and letters.
- **Programming** to write code.
- **Gaming** (with specialised gaming keyboards).
- **Controlling operating systems** with shortcuts and commands.
- **Data entry** in offices, schools, and businesses.

# 3.2.1.4 Optical Mouse

---

[![An illustration of an optical mouse with a cord, labeled 'Optical Mouse'.](images/topic3_hardware/3_2_1_input_devices/img_004.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-47.png?ssl=1)

An optical mouse is an **input device** used to control the movement of a pointer on a computer screen. Unlike older ball mice, it uses a **light source (usually an LED)** and a sensor to detect movement across a surface.

### Purpose of an Optical Mouse

The purpose of an optical mouse is to allow users to **interact with a graphical user interface (GUI)**. By moving the mouse, users can select, drag, and open items on the screen, making navigation easier and faster than using only a keyboard.

---

### How Does an Optical Mouse Work?

1. A small LED light shines onto the surface beneath the mouse.
2. A sensor captures many images per second of the surface.
3. The movement of the mouse changes the reflected light pattern.
4. The sensor and processor convert these changes into signals.
5. The computer interprets the signals and moves the pointer accordingly.

---

### Advantages of Optical Mice

- **More accurate** than traditional ball mice.
- Works on most flat surfaces without the need for a mouse pad.
- **Low maintenance** since there are no moving parts to clean.
- **Lightweight and portable** for use with laptops and desktops.

---

### Disadvantages of Optical Mice

- Struggles on **shiny or transparent surfaces** like glass.
- Requires a **flat surface** to work properly.
- Can use more **battery power** if wireless.
- Less precise than specialised devices like graphics tablets for drawing.

---

### Uses of Optical Mice

- **General computer navigation** in desktops and laptops.
- **Gaming** with high-precision models.
- **Design and editing software** for tasks requiring point-and-click control.
- **Education and office work** for daily operations like document editing and browsing.

# 3.2.1.5 Microphone

---

[![An icon representing a microphone, symbolizing an input device used for capturing audio.](images/topic3_hardware/3_2_1_input_devices/img_005.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-48.png?ssl=1)

### What is a Microphone?

A microphone is an **input device** that converts **sound waves** into digital signals so that a computer can process them. It captures audio such as voice, music, or environmental sounds and sends the data to the computer for storage, transmission, or further processing.

---

### Purpose of a Microphone

The purpose of a microphone is to allow **audio input** into a computer system. It enables communication, recording, and control through sound.

---

### How Does a Microphone Work?

1. Sound waves (vibrations in the air) reach the microphone.
2. The diaphragm inside the microphone vibrates when struck by sound waves.
3. These vibrations are converted into electrical signals.
4. The sound card or audio interface converts the signals into **digital data**.
5. The computer stores or processes the digital sound.

---

### Advantages of Microphones

- Enable **hands-free communication** in video calls or voice commands.
- Allow **sound recording** for music, podcasts, or videos.
- Useful in **accessibility**, e.g., speech recognition for disabled users.
- Can be built into many devices like laptops and phones.

---

### Disadvantages of Microphones

- **Background noise** can affect sound quality.
- Requires **additional software** for editing or processing.
- Quality depends on the type and price of the microphone.
- Can pick up **unwanted sounds**, leading to unclear recordings.

---

### Uses of Microphones

- **Voice recognition** for smart assistants and speech-to-text.
- **Video conferencing** and online learning.
- **Recording audio** for music, podcasts, or film.
- **Security systems**, such as intercoms and surveillance.
- **Gaming and streaming** for real-time communication.

---

# 3.2.1.6 Touch Screen

---

A touch screen is an **input device** that allows users to interact directly with a computer or digital device by touching the screen. Instead of using a keyboard or mouse, the user can tap, swipe, or drag on the display to give commands.

Touch screens are widely used in smartphones, tablets, ATMs, ticket machines, and interactive kiosks.

---

### Types of Touch Screens

## 3.2.1.6.1 Resistive Touch Screen

[![Illustration of a hand touching a resistive touch screen, labeled 'Resistive Touch Screen'.](images/topic3_hardware/3_2_1_input_devices/img_006.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-43.png?ssl=1)

A resistive touch screen is a type of **touch-sensitive input device** made up of two thin layers separated by a small gap. When you press the screen, the top layer bends and touches the bottom layer, completing a circuit. The system detects where the touch happened and sends that position to the computer.

### Purpose of a Resistive Touch Screen

The purpose of a resistive touch screen is to allow **direct interaction** with a device by touch. It is designed to work with almost any pointing object, such as a finger, stylus, or even a gloved hand.

---

### How Does a Resistive Touch Screen Work?

1. The screen has two flexible layers with a gap in between.
2. When pressure is applied, the layers make contact.
3. This closes an electrical circuit at the touch point.
4. The controller calculates the exact location of the touch.
5. The computer processes the input as a command.

---

### Advantages of Resistive Touch Screens

- **Works with any object** (finger, stylus, glove, etc.).
- **Low cost** compared to other touch screen types.
- Durable against dust and water.

---

### Disadvantages of Resistive Touch Screens

- Requires **pressure** to work – not as sensitive as capacitive.
- Cannot support **multi-touch** (e.g., pinch-to-zoom).
- Image clarity is lower because of multiple screen layers.
- Surface can wear out over time from repeated pressing.

---

### Uses of Resistive Touch Screens

- **ATMs** and cash machines.
- **Payment terminals** in shops.
- **Industrial equipment** where gloves are used.
- **Older smartphones and PDAs**.

---

## 3.2.1.6.2 Capacitive Touch Screen

---

[![An illustration of a capacitive touchscreen smartphone with a hand touching the screen, indicating touch interaction.](images/topic3_hardware/3_2_1_input_devices/img_007.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-41.png?ssl=1)

A capacitive touch screen is an **input device** that uses the natural electrical properties of the human body to detect touch. Instead of pressure, it relies on the ability of a finger to conduct electricity. When you touch the screen, it changes the electrical charge at that point, which is detected and processed by the device.

---

### Purpose of a Capacitive Touch Screen

The purpose of a capacitive touch screen is to provide a **sensitive, fast, and accurate** way of interacting with devices. It allows users to use **multi-touch gestures** such as swiping, pinching, and zooming, which are essential in modern smartphones and tablets.

---

### How Does a Capacitive Touch Screen Work?

1. The screen is coated with a transparent material that stores an electrical charge.
2. When a finger touches the screen, it draws a small amount of charge at that point.
3. Sensors at the screen corners detect the change in charge.
4. A controller calculates the exact position of the touch.
5. The computer interprets this as an input command.

---

### Advantages of Capacitive Touch Screens

- **Very sensitive** – responds quickly to light touches.
- Supports **multi-touch gestures** (e.g., zoom, swipe).
- **High clarity** – only one transparent layer is needed.
- **Durable** – does not wear out as quickly as resistive touch screens.

---

### Disadvantages of Capacitive Touch Screens

- Only works with **bare fingers** or a special stylus.
- **More expensive** than resistive touch screens.
- Can be affected by **moisture or sweat**, reducing accuracy.

---

### Uses of Capacitive Touch Screens

- **Smartphones** and **tablets**.
- **Modern laptops** and hybrid devices.
- **Touch-screen kiosks** in airports or shopping centres.
- **Gaming consoles** and handheld devices.

---

## 3.2.1.6.3 Infrared Touch Screen

---

[![Illustration of an infrared touchscreen device being activated by a finger, with concentric circles indicating touch interaction.](images/topic3_hardware/3_2_1_input_devices/img_008.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-42.png?ssl=1)

An infrared (IR) touch screen is an **input device** that uses invisible beams of infrared light to detect touch. Instead of pressure or electrical charge, it relies on **breaking light beams** across the surface of the screen. When a finger or object touches the screen, it interrupts the beams, and the system detects the exact location.

---

### Purpose of an Infrared Touch Screen

The purpose of an infrared touch screen is to provide a **durable and highly accurate** method of interacting with a device. Because it doesn’t rely on layers or coatings on the screen surface, the display image is very clear. It also works with almost any object, making it versatile in different environments.

---

### How Does an Infrared Touch Screen Work?

1. Infrared emitters and detectors are placed along the edges of the screen, creating a grid of invisible light beams.
2. When a finger or object touches the screen, it blocks some of the beams.
3. Sensors detect which beams are interrupted.
4. The controller calculates the exact touch location.
5. The computer interprets this as an input command.

---

### Advantages of Infrared Touch Screens

- **Very durable** – no pressure or physical contact needed between screen layers.
- **High clarity** – no extra layers, so the display is bright and clear.
- Works with **any object** (finger, stylus, glove).
- Supports **multi-touch** functionality.

---

### Disadvantages of Infrared Touch Screens

- **Expensive** compared to resistive and capacitive screens.
- **Dirt, dust, or sunlight** can interfere with the infrared beams.
- Slightly more complex to maintain in outdoor environments.

---

### Uses of Infrared Touch Screens

- **Large interactive displays** in schools, offices, and exhibitions.
- **Public information kiosks** in shopping centres or airports.
- **Industrial control systems** where gloves or tools may be used.
- **Gaming and entertainment systems** requiring multi-touch.

---

### Advantages of Touch Screens

- **Easy to use** – direct interaction.
- Combines **input and output** in one device.
- Saves space (no need for separate mouse/keyboard).
- Supports gestures like zooming and swiping.

---

### Disadvantages of Touch Screens

- Can be **expensive** compared to other input devices.
- Screen may get **scratched or dirty** from constant touching.
- Not ideal for long typing tasks.
- Can be less accurate than a mouse for detailed tasks.

---

# 3.2.1.7 Digital Camera

---

[![Icon of a digital camera with the text 'Digital camera' beneath it.](images/topic3_hardware/3_2_1_input_devices/img_010.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-49.png?ssl=1)

### What is a Digital Camera?

A digital camera is an **input device** that captures photographs and videos in a digital format. Instead of using film, it uses an **image sensor** to convert light into electrical signals, which are then stored as digital files (e.g., JPEG, PNG).

### Purpose of a Digital Camera

The purpose of a digital camera is to **record visual information** that can be stored, edited, and shared using a computer or other devices. Unlike traditional cameras, digital cameras allow **instant viewing and editing** of images.

---

### How Does a Digital Camera Work?

1. Light enters through the **camera lens**.
2. The light is focused onto an **image sensor** (CCD or CMOS).
3. The sensor converts the light into electrical signals.
4. The camera’s processor changes the signals into a **digital image file**.
5. The image is stored in the camera’s memory card and can be transferred to a computer.

---

### Advantages of Digital Cameras

- **Instant access** to captured photos and videos.
- Images can be **stored, shared, and edited** easily.
- High-quality images with good detail.
- Can record both **still images and video**.
- More environmentally friendly than film cameras (no film/chemicals needed).

---

### Disadvantages of Digital Cameras

- Can be **expensive**, especially professional models.
- Require **battery power** to operate.
- Large files may need significant **storage space**.
- Image quality depends on sensor size and lens quality.

---

### Uses of Digital Cameras

- **Personal photography** and social media.
- **Security systems** (CCTV, surveillance).
- **Professional photography** in media and design.
- **Medical imaging** (specialised cameras).
- **Education and research** to capture evidence and experiments.

---

# 3.2.1.8 2D Scanner

---

[![Icon of a 2D scanner with an open lid and barcode pattern on the scanning surface.](images/topic3_hardware/3_2_1_input_devices/img_011.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-50.png?ssl=1)

### What is a 2D Scanner?

A 2D scanner is an **input device** used to make a **digital copy of flat images or documents**. It captures text, drawings, and photos by shining light on the surface and converting the reflected light into a digital file.

### Purpose of a 2D Scanner

The purpose of a 2D scanner is to **digitise physical documents or images** so they can be stored, edited, or shared on a computer. This reduces the need to keep paper copies and allows for easy processing of information.

---

### How Does a 2D Scanner Work?

1. The document or photo is placed on the scanner’s glass surface.
2. A light source (usually LED) shines onto the document.
3. Sensors (CCD or CIS) detect the reflected light from the image.
4. The light is converted into electrical signals.
5. The scanner’s processor converts these signals into a **digital image file** (e.g., PDF, JPEG).

---

### Advantages of 2D Scanners

- **Accurate reproduction** of documents and images.
- Allows **easy storage and sharing** of documents digitally.
- Can be combined with **OCR (Optical Character Recognition)** software to convert scanned text into editable text.
- Useful for creating **backups** of important documents.

---

### Disadvantages of 2D Scanners

- Produces **large file sizes** if scanning high-quality images.
- **Slower** compared to typing or taking digital photos.
- Quality of scanned image depends on the **resolution** of the scanner.
- Limited to **flat objects** (cannot scan 3D items).

---

### Uses of 2D Scanners

- **Digitising photographs** and artwork.
- **Scanning documents** in offices, schools, and libraries.
- **Passport and ID scanning** at airports.
- **Banking** – scanning cheques.
- **Legal and healthcare** – storing official records digitally.

---

# 3.2.1.9 3D Scanner

---

[![Illustration of a 3D scanner with a light source projecting onto an object, symbolizing the process of capturing three-dimensional shapes.](images/topic3_hardware/3_2_1_input_devices/img_012.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-51.png?ssl=1)

### What is a 3D Scanner?

A 3D scanner is an **input device** that captures the **shape and appearance of real-world objects** to create a digital 3D model. Instead of scanning flat images like a 2D scanner, it records the **geometry, texture, and sometimes colour** of an object.

---

### Purpose of a 3D Scanner

The purpose of a 3D scanner is to **digitise physical objects** so they can be viewed, modified, tested, or reproduced using a computer. This is especially useful in **design, manufacturing, medicine, and research**.

---

### How Does a 3D Scanner Work?

1. The scanner projects a light (laser or structured light) onto the object.
2. Sensors measure how the light bounces back from the object’s surface.
3. The scanner calculates the **shape, distance, and contours** of the object.
4. The collected data is converted into a **3D digital model**.
5. The model can then be edited, stored, or even printed using a 3D printer.

---

### Advantages of 3D Scanners

- Can capture **complex shapes** that are difficult to measure manually.
- Produces **accurate digital models** for design and testing.
- Saves time in **engineering and prototyping**.
- Allows **reverse engineering** of existing products.

---

### Disadvantages of 3D Scanners

- **Expensive** compared to 2D scanners.
- Requires **powerful computers** to process 3D data.
- Scanning may be **slow** for large or detailed objects.
- File sizes are often **very large**.

---

### Uses of 3D Scanners

- **Manufacturing and engineering** – designing and testing products.
- **Medical field** – creating models for prosthetics or implants.
- **Cultural heritage** – preserving historical artefacts in digital form.
- **Animation and gaming** – creating 3D characters and environments.
- **Quality control** – checking manufactured products against design specifications.

---

# **Common Scenarios and Suitable Input Devices**

### **1. Recording sound**

**Scenario:** A student records himself singing for a music project.  
**Input device:** **Microphone**  
**Reason:** A microphone captures sound and converts it into digital data.

### **2. Taking or inputting images**

**Scenario:** A photo is taken and transferred to a computer.  
**Input device:** **Digital camera**  
**Reason:** A digital camera captures images from the real world and inputs them into the computer.

### **3. Entering text or numbers**

**Scenario:** A user types a password, name, or membership number.  
**Input device:** **Keyboard** or **Touch screen**  
**Reason:** These devices allow manual entry of text and numbers.

### **4. Automatic ticket checking**

**Scenario:** A train station checks tickets automatically.  
**Input devices:** **Barcode scanner**, **QR code scanner**, **Smart card reader**  
**Reason:** These devices automatically read ticket data without manual input.

### **5. Scanning documents**

**Scenario:** A paper document needs to be stored digitally.  
**Input device:** **2D scanner**  
**Reason:** A scanner captures images of documents and converts them into digital form.

### **6. Capturing real-world movement or position**

**Scenario:** A phone detects screen rotation or steps taken.  
**Input device:** **Accelerometer**  
**Reason:** It senses movement and inputs data automatically.

### **7. Recording video**

**Scenario:** A short video is recorded for a project.  
**Input device:** **Digital camera**  
**Reason:** It captures moving images and inputs them into the computer.

### **8. Using touch-based systems**

**Scenario:** A self-checkout machine in a supermarket.  
**Input device:** **Touch screen**  
**Reason:** Users select options directly by touching the display.
