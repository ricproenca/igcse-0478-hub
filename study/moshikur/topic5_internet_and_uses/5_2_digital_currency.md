# 5.2.1

# Digital currency

## concept and uses

[![An illustration depicting digital currency concepts, featuring a smartphone displaying a dollar bill symbol, a digital coin with a 'B' representing Bitcoin, and additional Bitcoin symbols around it.](images/topic5_internet_and_uses/5_2_digital_currency/img_001.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-106.png?ssl=1)

Digital currencies are a relatively new but rapidly growing part of the global financial system. This tutorial will help you understand the concept of digital currency, how it is used, and the technology behind it—blockchain.

### What is a digital currency?

A digital currency is money that exists only in electronic form.  
There are no paper notes or metal coins to hold.  
Values are stored as numbers inside computer systems.

Digital currency can be:

- the balance in an online account or payment app
- a currency created for use inside a game or platform
- a cryptocurrency created and stored using computer systems

The key idea is that all records are electronic and updates happen by computers.

---

### Where the money is kept

- Digital currency is stored in a software account often called a digital wallet.
- The wallet can be part of a bank app, payment app, game account, or a cryptocurrency wallet.
- Access is protected using passwords, PINs, two-factor codes, or device security.

---

### How it is used

1. A user chooses to pay with digital currency in an app or on a website.
2. The user confirms the payment (for example by entering a PIN, password, or using biometric login).
3. The system subtracts the amount from the sender’s wallet and adds it to the receiver’s wallet.
4. Both accounts show updated balances immediately or within a short time.
5. A receipt or transaction reference is stored electronically.

---

### Key Characteristics of Digital Currency

1. **Electronic Form Only**: Digital currencies do not have a physical form, such as coins or notes. They exist entirely in a digital environment.
2. **Global Accessibility**: Digital currencies can be used anywhere in the world, provided there is Internet access.
3. **Decentralized or Centralized**:
   - Some digital currencies, like Bitcoin, are **decentralized**, meaning they are not controlled by a central authority (like a government or bank).
   - Others, such as digital versions of national currencies, are **centralized** and controlled by a central bank.
4. **Transactions and Ownership**:
   - Transactions are recorded electronically and can occur instantly.
   - Ownership is often represented by a digital wallet, which acts like a bank account for storing digital currency.

---

### Examples of Digital Currencies

1. **Bitcoin**: The first decentralized cryptocurrency.
2. **Ethereum**: A platform that supports smart contracts and its native cryptocurrency, Ether.
3. **Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs)**: Digital forms of a country’s national currency, such as China’s digital yuan.

### Typical uses

- Buying goods and services online
- Paying in apps, streaming services, and games
- Contactless or QR payments in shops when the phone is linked to a wallet
- Sending money to friends and family
- Micropayments such as paying for a single article or small in-game item
- International transfers without using cash

---

### Benefits

- Fast transfer of money between users
- No need to handle physical cash
- Works well for online shopping and app purchases
- Easy to store small amounts and make small payments
- Electronic records make it easy to check past payments

---

### Considerations

- Requires a device and internet access
- Users must keep login details secure
- If access credentials are lost or stolen, funds can be at risk
- Some systems charge fees for transfers or currency conversion

---

### Simple examples

- Using a phone wallet to pay for bus fare
- Buying a book from an online store using a payment app balance
- Purchasing an in-game item using the game’s digital currency
- Sending money to a friend’s wallet after splitting a bill

# 5.2.2

# Blockchain and its Role in Digital Currency

[![Graphic illustrating the concept of blockchain, featuring a chain link design with the word 'BLOCKCHAIN' and a placeholder for a website URL.](images/topic5_internet_and_uses/5_2_digital_currency/img_002.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image-107.png?ssl=1)

---

### What is Blockchain?

Blockchain is a **digital ledger** used to record digital currency transactions.  
It stores data in units called **blocks**, and each block is linked to the one before it, forming a continuous **chain**.  
This makes blockchain secure, permanent, and very difficult to tamper with.

---

### The Genesis Block

The **genesis block** is the very first block in a blockchain.

- It is the starting point of the entire chain.
- All other blocks are linked back to this first block.
- Without the genesis block, the blockchain cannot exist, because it is the foundation of the chain.

---

### Contents of a Block

Each block in the blockchain contains important information:

1. **Transaction details**
   - Records of the sender, receiver, and the amount of digital currency transferred.
2. **Time-stamp**
   - The exact date and time the block was created.
3. **Hash of the previous block**
   - A unique code that links the block to the one before it.
   - This creates the “chain” in blockchain.
4. **Block hash**
   - A unique code that identifies the block itself.
   - If any data inside the block is changed, the hash changes, making tampering obvious.
5. **Other metadata**
   - Information needed by the system to keep the blockchain secure and consistent across all computers.

---

### How Blockchain Works

1. A digital currency transaction is made (for example, a payment).
2. The transaction is grouped with others into a new block.
3. The block is given a time-stamp and contains the hash of the previous block.
4. Computers on the network verify the block.
5. Once verified, the block is added permanently to the chain.
6. The chain grows longer, with every block linking back to the **genesis block**.

---

### Key Features

- **Decentralised**: Stored across many computers, not controlled by one person or organisation.
- **Permanent**: Once a block is added, it cannot be changed or deleted.
- **Time-stamped**: Every transaction has a clear date and time.
- **Linked**: Each block depends on the previous block, forming a secure chain.

---

### Importance for Digital Currency

- Prevents double spending of the same currency.
- Builds trust since all transactions are recorded and visible.
- Protects against fraud because altering data would break the chain.
- Provides a permanent, traceable history of transactions.

---

### Example

Think of blockchain like a history book:

- The **genesis block** is the very first page.
- Each new page (block) contains details of events (transactions), the date (time-stamp), and a reference to the page before it.
- If someone tried to change a page, it would no longer match the references in later pages, and everyone would see the mistake.
