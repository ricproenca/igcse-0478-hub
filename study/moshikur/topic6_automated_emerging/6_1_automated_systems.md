## **What Are Automated Systems?**

An automated system is a system that can perform tasks on its own, with little or no human help. These systems use **technology** to monitor the environment, process information, and act based on the data they receive.

[![Illustration of automated systems in a warehouse setting, featuring robotic arms sorting boxes on a conveyor belt, a checkout station with a 'Scan Items' screen, and shelves filled with packages. The text highlights performing tasks automatically without human intervention.](images/topic6_automated_emerging/6_1_automated_systems/img_001.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/image-17.png?ssl=1)

**Examples:**

Automatic doors that open when you approach.

Smart thermostats that adjust room temperature.

Robots used in factories to assemble products.

## **Sensors**

**What they do:** Sensors detect changes in the environment, like temperature, light, or motion.

**Example:** A motion sensor detects if someone is in a room.

**Why they are important:** They provide the system with information it needs to make decisions.

## **Microprocessors**

**What they do:** The microprocessor processes the data from the sensors and decides what to do next.

**Example:** After receiving data from a motion sensor, a microprocessor might decide to turn the lights on.

**Why they are important:** They make the decisions based on the programmed rules.

## **Actuators**

**What they do:** Actuators carry out actions, like turning a motor or opening a valve.

**Example:** An actuator might turn on a fan if the room gets too hot.

**Why they are important:** They make the system take real-world actions.

---

## **How Automated Systems Work**

1. **Step 1: Sensing**  
   The **sensor** detects a change in the environment. For example, a soil moisture sensor measures how much water is in the soil.
2. **Step 2: Processing**  
   The **microprocessor** takes the sensor’s data and compares it to a set of programmed rules. For example, the microprocessor may check if the soil is dry.
3. **Step 3: Acting**  
   If the rules are met, the **actuator** carries out an action. For example, the actuator might turn on a water pump to water the soil.

---

## **Advantages of Automated Systems**

Automated systems are widely used because they make tasks easier. Here are some key benefits:

1. **Saves time**: Systems can work faster than humans.
2. **Increases accuracy**: They reduce mistakes, especially in repetitive tasks.
3. **Works continuously**: Unlike humans, they don’t get tired and can work 24/7.
4. **Reduces costs**: Once set up, they can save money by replacing manual work.

**Example:** In a factory, robots can assemble products quickly and with fewer mistakes than humans.

---

## **Disadvantages of Automated Systems**

While automated systems are helpful, they also have some drawbacks:

1. **High initial cost**: Installing and programming the system can be expensive.
2. **Dependence on technology**: If the system breaks down, everything stops until it is fixed.
3. **Job loss**: They can replace jobs that were done by humans.
4. **Complex maintenance**: Skilled workers are needed to repair and maintain them.

**Example:** If a robot in a car factory malfunctions, production might stop until it is repaired.

---

## **Examples of Automated Systems in Real Life**

1. **Industry**: Robots assemble cars in factories.
2. **Agriculture**: Automated irrigation systems water crops based on soil moisture.
3. **Gaming**: AI opponents in video games adapt to how the player plays.
4. **Lighting**: Smart lights turn on and off based on room occupancy.
5. **Science**: Automated telescopes track stars and planets without human help.
