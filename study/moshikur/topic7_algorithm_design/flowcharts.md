# 5.4 Algorithm – Flowchart

---

[![Flow chart legend with labeled shapes: Flow Line, Process, Subroutine, Input/Output, Decision, and Terminator.](images/topic7_algorithm_design/flowcharts/img_001.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/image-6.png?ssl=1)

Flowcharts are diagrams that show **how a process or algorithm works**, step by step. Each step is shown using a **specific symbol**.

The **Cambridge IGCSE/O Level Computer Science (0478/2210) syllabus** includes flowcharts as part of **Paper 2: Algorithms, Programming and Logic**.

Here’s **exactly what the syllabus says about flowcharts**:

### Understand and **use standard flowchart symbols**:

- **Terminator** – Start/Stop (oval)
- **Process** – instruction or calculation (rectangle)
- **Decision** – a condition with Yes/No or True/False outcomes (diamond)
- **Input/Output** – for reading data or displaying results (parallelogram)
- **Subroutine** – calling a procedure (rectangle with double vertical lines)
- **Arrow** – shows the direction of flow (line/arrow)

### Construct and interpret **flowcharts** to solve a given problem or represent logic clearly.

Here are the **6 flowchart symbols** you need to know for your IGCSE/O Level exam:

### 1. **Start/Stop Symbol**

- **Shape:** Oval (also called a terminator)
- **Use:** Shows where the algorithm **starts or ends**.
- **Example:** `Start`

---

[![Button with text 'SET Total TO 0' on a white background](images/topic7_algorithm_design/flowcharts/img_003.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/image-10.png?ssl=1)

### 2. **Process Symbol**

- **Shape:** Rectangle
- **Use:** Shows a **step or instruction** to do something.
- **Example:** `Total ← Num1 + Num2`

---

[![Flowchart asking if x is greater than 0, with 'YES' and 'NO' options.](images/topic7_algorithm_design/flowcharts/img_004.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/image-11.png?ssl=1)

### 3. **Decision Symbol**

- **Shape:** Diamond
- **Use:** Used for a **question or condition** with **Yes/No** or **True/False** answers.
- **Example:** `Is Num > 100?`

---

[![Graphic featuring the word 'OUTPUT' in bold letters, accompanied by a stylized 'x' on a white background.](images/topic7_algorithm_design/flowcharts/img_005.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/image-12.png?ssl=1)

### 4. **Input/Output Symbol**

- **Shape:** Parallelogram
- **Use:** Used for **reading input** from the user or **showing output**.
- **Example:** `INPUT Num OUTPUT "Total is", Total`

---

[![A blank white canvas with black borders.](images/topic7_algorithm_design/flowcharts/img_006.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/image-13.png?ssl=1)

### 5. **Subroutine Symbol**

- **Shape:** Rectangle with double vertical lines at the sides
- **Use:** Calls a **subroutine** or a predefined set of instructions.
- **Example:** `Call CalculateTotal`

---

[![Right-facing arrow graphic](images/topic7_algorithm_design/flowcharts/img_007.png)](https://i0.wp.com/moshikur.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/image-14.png?ssl=1)

### 6. **Arrow**

- **Shape:** Arrow line (→ ↓ etc.)
- **Use:** **Connects the symbols** and shows the **direction** of the flow.

- A **fail** is a mark **less than 50**
- Count how many marks are **passes**
- Count how many marks are **fails**
- After all 10 marks have been entered, output:
  - the number of passes
  - the number of fails

- Input **five numbers**
- Add the five numbers together
- Calculate the **average** of the five numbers

## Task 15

- Input **five numbers**
- Each number may be positive, negative, or zero
- Add **only the positive numbers** to a running total
- Ignore any numbers that are zero or negative
- After all five numbers have been entered, output the **sum of the positive numbers**.
